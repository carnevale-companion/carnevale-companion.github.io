## Discipline Role

Each discipline defines a Mage's role in the gang, since all spells must come from the same school. Analysis below covers the role each discipline creates, what makes it strong, and what it cannot do.

### Blood Rites

**Theme:** Direct harm. LP drain. Armour penetration. Every spell either deals damage, strips LP, or enables a model to deal more damage.

**What it does well:**

- The only discipline with meaningful Penetration on multiple spells (Pen -2 to -4). Against armoured targets, no other discipline matches its effective damage output.

**What it cannot do:**

- No utility except Abyssal Mist, but it has high difficulty and is expensive.

**Role in the gang:** An agressive direct damage dealer.

---

### Divinity

**Theme:** Sustain, protection, and punishment of enemy Mages.

**What it does well:**

- The best and most varied healing toolkit.
- Defensive single-target and AoE buffs.
- Cantrip of the Sun partially offsets WP expenditure — the discipline's WP economy is better than raw costs suggest.
- Holy Light is the only anti-Mage AoE in the game.

**What it cannot do:**

- Zero offensive output against non-magic opponents.
- Three of seven spells are at Difficulty 8 (Holy Light, Defender of Destiny, Aqua Curitiva) — the highest concentration of hard-to-land spells of any discipline.

**Role in the gang:** The best protector and healer, a full support mage with a situationnal anti-mage role.

---

### Fateweaving

**Theme:** Stat manipulation. Dice re-rolls. Fateweaving does not deal damage or heal — it makes everything else more likely to succeed.

**What it does well:**

- The only discipline that directly manipulates dice results.
- Flexible stat buffs/debuffs.
- Glimpse of Glory grants the highest single-model combat buff package in the game.
- Marksman's Fortune and Otherworldly Oddity provide niche utility unavailable anywhere else (Reload manipulation, terrain control).

**What it cannot do:**

- Zero direct damage or healing.
- Value is entirely dependent on having high-quality models to buff or debuff.

**Role in the gang:** A force multiplier that makes the gang's best models perform above their baseline, or a debuffer that hinders enemy models.

---

### Runes of Sovereignty — Control and action economy

**Theme:** Dominance over movement and actions, support or control by moving people around.

**What it does well:**

- Madness is the highest-ceiling single spell in the game and fully irreplaceable.
- Cantrip of the Chariot and Fiery Rhetoric are the only spell-based sources of free movement and CP recovery.
- Wave of Force is a flexible long range effect that move opponents and allies alike.

**What it cannot do:**

- Zero direct damage or healing.
- Very short range or spells needing water to function.

**Role in the gang:** A disruptive controller or a movement based support. Must be able to survive or be mobile enough to be where it needs to be.

---

### Wild Magic — Versatile generalist

**Theme:** Adaptability. Wild Magic covers damage, healing, debuffing, movement, teleportation, and spell-copying in a single school.

**What it does well:**

- Cantrip of Justice is a unique way to expand the spells known by the caster.
- Summon Vermin is the best 1-WP damage spell on expected value, with the longest range of any damage spell.
- Sunder Armour is the best force-multiplier setup spell, enabling devastating follow-up attacks by Summon Vermin or any friendly model.
- Gateway is a very flexible and powerful movement spell.

**What it cannot do:**

- Very narrow buff spell.
- Not the best at anything

**Role in the gang:** A flexible hybrid that adapts to what the gang lacks, very dependant of its base profile.
